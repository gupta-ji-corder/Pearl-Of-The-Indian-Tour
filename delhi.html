Places to Visit in Delhi

Red Fort

Red Fort is best known for its majestic architecture in India, captivating history buffs and travelers alike with a vibrant blend of Mughal design and cultural heritage. It is also one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the world, standing proudly in the heart of Old Delhi as a symbol of India’s rich past. Tourists from across the globe flock to this magnificent fort, which is enclosed by massive red sandstone walls and dotted with domed structures, lush gardens, and intricate marble inlays. All of it forms a key part of the historic landscape of the capital city of India. You need to arrive early in the day to truly soak in the grandeur of this monument before the crowds gather and the midday heat sets in. Make sure you're ready to explore the fort by 9 am, or you might miss the tranquil morning atmosphere that enhances its charm. Wander through the inner chambers of the Red Fort to discover Diwan-i-Aam and Diwan-i-Khas, the royal halls that echo with centuries of history and royal decree. You’ll encounter ornately carved pavilions, a museum showcasing Mughal artifacts, and sweeping views of the bustling city beyond the fort’s high walls. Beyond its architectural splendor, the Red Fort is surrounded by bustling bazaars and cultural corners, offering a peek into the vibrant life of Delhi. The fort represents a living piece of history and is considered a national treasure for its cultural, political, and historical significance.

Qutub Minar

Tucked within the historic folds of Delhi’s Mehrauli area, Qutub Minar once stood as a towering symbol of the early Delhi Sultanate’s architectural legacy. The grandeur of this minaret and the intricacy of its stone carvings have made it one of the most striking and visited tourist attractions in India. Qutub Minar is situated close to several other monuments of the Qutub Complex and is entirely surrounded by lush gardens and age-old ruins that whisper stories of India’s medieval past. Crowning itself as one of the tallest brick minarets in the world, Qutub Minar attracts both history buffs and architecture enthusiasts for a wide range of experiences. The Iron Pillar, Alai Darwaza, Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque, and the unfinished Alai Minar are some key highlights within the Qutub Complex. In recognition of its cultural and historical importance, Qutub Minar was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993. Today, what you witness is not just a towering monument, but a layered narrative of dynasties and empires that shaped northern India. Each intricately carved sandstone and marble detail tells a story of conquest, faith, and craftsmanship.

Humayun's Tomb,

Humayun’s Tomb is a grand architectural marvel built in 1570 by Empress Bega Begum, the wife of the Mughal Emperor Humayun. This impressive mausoleum, situated in the heart of Delhi, is often referred to as the precursor to the Taj Mahal for its magnificent Persian-style domes and symmetrical gardens. Sprawling over a vast complex of around 27 hectares, the tomb is surrounded by lush green lawns and a classic Charbagh-style garden, making it one of the most serene retreats in the bustling city. The tomb itself is built on a raised platform and crowned with a magnificent double dome, showcasing intricate red sandstone and white marble work. At its core lies the central chamber housing the emperor’s cenotaph, while smaller chambers around it hold the graves of several other Mughal royals. The site is also dotted with pavilions, water channels, and walkways that offer visitors peaceful spots to sit, relax, and admire the artistry of the Mughal era. Tourists and history enthusiasts often stroll along the garden pathways, especially in the morning or evening when the weather is pleasant, and the setting sun casts a golden hue on the sandstone structure. The complex is particularly captivating at dusk, as the tomb glows softly under the fading light. Photography lovers and architecture buffs find themselves drawn to its balance, symmetry, and ornamentation.

Delhi View Point

The mesmerizing Delhi Ridge viewpoint is located near the Flagstaff Tower on Kamla Nehru Ridge and is ranked among the top 10 offbeat attractions in Delhi. Nestled within the Northern Ridge Forest, this viewpoint offers a peaceful retreat surrounded by ancient trees, serene meadows, and panoramic cityscapes. From the heart of the city, you need to take the Rani Jhansi Road or the Delhi University metro route to reach the ridge area. A short 800-meter uphill walk through shaded forest trails leads to the Flagstaff Tower viewpoint. If you prefer comfort, a cycle rickshaw or e-rickshaw from the University metro station can bring you closer to the entry point. From the top, you’ll witness a sweeping view of Old Delhi’s skyline, with landmarks such as Jama Masjid, Red Fort, and the Yamuna River visible in the distance. The contrast between the lush greenery and historic monuments makes the climb absolutely worth it.

Highlights: Elevated points give scenic views of the city’s blend of nature and architecture.

Location:Sundar Nagar, New Delhi

Timings:Anytime during the day.

National Museum

Located at Janpath, the grand National Museum of Delhi, established in 1949, is one of the most prominent cultural attractions of India’s capital city. The museum is housed in an expansive building spanning multiple floors, each dedicated to showcasing the vast and varied heritage of the Indian subcontinent—from prehistoric times to modern history. The ground floor features ancient sculptures, relics of the Indus Valley Civilization, and intricate artifacts from early Indian dynasties. Moving up, the upper floors exhibit stunning collections of manuscripts, tribal art, decorative pieces, and weapons used by legendary rulers. For those interested in purchasing books, souvenirs, and replicas of artifacts, the museum includes a well-stocked gift shop that caters to both casual visitors and dedicated history lovers. The museum also features a reading room and a reference library, ideal for scholars and curious minds alike.

Location: Janpath, New Delh

Price: Rs. 20 for Indian adults and Rs. 650 for foreign nationals(approx)

Best Time: Tuesday to Sunday, 10 am to 6 pm
